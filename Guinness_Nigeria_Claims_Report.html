8. Recommendations & Next Steps

Recommended Actions

  • Provider Audit — Lily Hospital Benin: With ₦23.1M across 244 members (10.9% of portfolio), conduct a tariff review and utilisation audit. Negotiate volume discounts or consider steering to alternative Benin providers.
  • High-Cost Case Management: Outreach Signature (₦7.2M, 1 member — neonatal), HIGHENDUROLOGY (₦3M, 1 member — urology), and POSH (₦3.3M, 17 members) require pre-authorization tightening and case management protocols.
  • Surgical Cost Control: Surgery is 6.6% of claims but driven by few high-value cases. Strengthen surgical pre-auth with second-opinion requirements for procedures >₦500K.
  • Medication Spend (20.8% of claims): Review branded vs generic prescribing patterns. Consider expanding Pharmacy Benefit Programme to control medication costs.
  • Chronic Disease Management: ₦8.4M in chronic medication — introduce wellness programmes and medication adherence tracking to reduce acute exacerbations.
  • Renewal Premium Review: Current premium of ₦157,433/head is insufficient. With projected claims at ₦167,292/head, a minimum 30-35% premium increase is required to achieve break-even COR, or the benefit structure must be restructured.
